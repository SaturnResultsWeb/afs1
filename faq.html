Frequently asked
questions.

Everything you need to know before you sail — booking, luggage, pets, and check-in.

Our skippers are constantly checking the weather forecast, and we will give you as much notice as possible if we believe your sailing might be delayed or cancelled. We will either refund the sailing or rebook you onto a different date.

Cancellations made over 48 hours before travel receive a refund minus £10 per person per leg (£20 return). Should you cancel after the 48 hour limit, no refunds are made — this also applies if a passenger fails to sail as booked.

Contact us at info@alderneyferry.com with your requested changes. Bookings can be changed up to 48 hours before the scheduled sailing time, subject to availability — an administration fee may apply.

Please arrive 30 minutes before departure. In Alderney, wait at the walled area near the Alderney Sailing Club. In Guernsey, wait by the inter-island pontoon. For Cherbourg check-in, see the entry below.

Check-in is at Bureau du port de plaisance Port Chantereyne, 50100 Cherbourg-en-Cotentin, France. Our crew will assist with passport control at J or K pontoon. EU passport holders skip this step and can wait onboard.

Dogs are welcome at £5 per leg. They are allowed to sit with you in the cabin on the seat next to you, and don't require muzzles unless needed for safety.

Bicycles and e-bikes are accepted with a tiered charge. You travel with your bikes at your own risk, and they will be stored outside on the deck, secured to the railings.

All sporting equipment is carried free of charge, provided passengers can manage it independently getting on and off the boat.

There's no fixed luggage limit — passengers may bring items free of charge if they can transport them themselves.

Children over 11 may travel alone. They must be seated indoors at all times, next to the skipper. Parents are responsible for arranging drop-off and pickup.
